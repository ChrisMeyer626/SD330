Six Processes of Cognition:

Attention-

It is central to life and enables us to concentrate on what is prevelent to us and warrants our effort.

  • Consider context

  • Use techniques to achieve this when designing visual interfaces

  • Avoid cluttering visual interfaces with too much information

  • Consider designing different ways of supporting effective switching and returning to a particular interface

Perception-

Refers to how information is acquired from the environment via the five sense organs(vision, hearing, taste, smell, and touch)

  • Design icons and other graphical representations so that users can readily distinguish between them

  • Obvious seperators and white space are effective visual methods for grouping information that make it easier to perceive and locate items

  • Design audio to be readily distinguishable from one another so that users can perceive how they differ and remember what each one represents.

  • Research proper color contrast techniques when designing an interface

  • Haptic feedback should be used judiciously

Memory-

Involves recalling various kinds of knowledge that allow people to act appropriately

  • Reduce cognitive load by avoiding long and complicated procedures for carrying out tasks

  • Design interfaces that promote recognition rather than recall by using familiar interaction patterns

  • Provide users with a variety of ways of labeling digital information

Learning-

Being closely connected to memeory,it involves the accumulation of skills and knowledge that would be impossible to achieve with memory

  • Design interfaces that encourage exploration

  • Design interfaces that constrain and guide users to select appropriate actions when initially learning

Reading, Speaking, & Listening-

Three forms of language processing that have some similar and some different properties

  • Keep the length of speech-based menus and instructions to a minimum

  • Accentuate the intonation of artifically generated speech voices, as they are harder to understand than human voices

  • Provide people with opportunities for making text large on a screen

Problem-Solving, Reasoning, & Decision-Making

Processes involving reflective cognition and include thinking about what to do, what the available options are, and the consequences brought by said actions

  • Provide information and help pages that are easy to access for people who want to understand more about how to carry out an activity more effectivly

  • Use simple and memorable functions to support rapid decision-making and planning
